## 🌍 Introduction

Attending the MapBiomas Info Session at ICPAC was an insightful experience that brought together technology, data, and environmental conservation. As a geospatial enthusiast, this session opened my eyes to the power of collaborative mapping and how data can drive sustainable decision-making.

## 🏢 At ICPAC: The Experience

Being at ICPAC provided a professional and inspiring environment filled with experts and learners passionate about climate and geospatial science.

What stood out for me:

- A collaborative learning atmosphere
- Interaction with like-minded geospatial professionals
- Exposure to real-world climate and environmental data applications

It felt like being part of a community working toward a shared goal—understanding and protecting our environment.

## 🗺️ Understanding MapBiomas

The session introduced MapBiomas as a platform that leverages satellite imagery and advanced algorithms to map land use and land cover changes over time.

Key highlights:

- Use of satellite data for large-scale mapping
- Continuous monitoring of environmental changes
- Open-access data for research and decision-making

This reinforced the importance of accessible geospatial data in solving environmental challenges.

## 🛰️ The Role of Technology

One of the most exciting parts was seeing how technology powers MapBiomas.

Technologies discussed:

- Remote sensing and satellite imagery
- Cloud computing platforms like Google Earth Engine
- Machine Learning for land classification

This connected directly with my interests in GIS and data-driven environmental analysis.

## 🚧 Key Takeaways

The session left me with valuable insights:

- The importance of collaboration in geospatial projects
- The power of open data in driving impact
- The growing role of AI and ML in environmental monitoring

It also highlighted how skills in GIS and data science can contribute to climate action.

## 🚀 The Future and My Perspective

This experience strengthened my interest in applying geospatial technologies for environmental conservation.

Looking ahead, I am excited about:

- Using satellite data for real-world impact
- Contributing to environmental monitoring projects
- Exploring platforms like MapBiomas in my own work

For me, this was more than just a session—it was a step forward in my journey of using data and technology to address environmental challenges.
